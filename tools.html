TOOLS

1. Digital Power Meter

The digital power meter is a hardware tool used to measure the real-time power consumption of devices by recording their watt usage across different states (idle, low load, and high load). This device provides precise readings by capturing the power drawn by each device every second, helping us identify peak energy usage and potential areas for improvement. The digital power meter is especially useful in gauging overall consumption and tracking variations in power use under different operating conditions.

2. Clamp Meter

The clamp meter is used to measure the current flow, allowing for component-level power analysis. By clamping onto the power supply line, this tool provides insights into power usage by individual components, such as processors or cooling fans. The clamp meter is invaluable for understanding how specific parts contribute to the total power consumption of a device.

3. Intel Power Gadget

Intel Power Gadget is a software-based tool that monitors the power consumption of Intel processors, providing a breakdown of energy usage by CPU activity. This tool tracks consumption across various tasks, offering real-time data that helps us analyze how different computing activities, such as video editing or gaming, impact overall power draw.

4. Microsoft Joulemeter

Microsoft Joulemeter estimates the energy consumption of computing devices based on usage patterns and component specifications. This software helps capture additional power usage details that may not be visible through hardware tools, making it useful for identifying which components consume the most energy and how various settings affect device efficiency.
