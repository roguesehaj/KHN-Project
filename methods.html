VIDEOS AND DOCUMENTARIES

Device Selection and Specifications

We analyzed three types of computing devices—desktops, laptops, and servers—each chosen for its significance in common usage scenarios. The desktop represents standard office setups, the laptop covers portable, energy-efficient devices, and the server highlights data-intensive applications in enterprise environments. Testing devices with comparable specifications allows us to draw meaningful conclusions about energy efficiency across different setups.

Operating Conditions Tested

Each device was assessed under four main operating conditions: idle (powered on with no active applications), low load (light tasks like browsing), high load (intensive tasks like gaming), and power-saving mode (sleep or low-power state). Testing these conditions gives us insights into how each state affects energy consumption, revealing patterns of high and low energy demand that are crucial for effective power management.

Experimental Design

We conducted the tests in a controlled environment, maintaining a consistent room temperature to prevent fluctuations in energy data. Each device was tested for 30 minutes to 1 hour per condition, and power consumption was recorded every minute. This systematic approach ensures reliable and comparable results across devices and conditions.

Proposed Efficiency Methods

We tested several methods for reducing energy consumption, including lowering screen brightness on laptops, activating low-power modes, and limiting server CPU performance to 70%. These methods proved effective in reducing power usage, with minimal impact on device performance. These efficiency strategies, when implemented broadly, can lead to significant reductions in overall energy consumption.
