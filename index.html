INTRODUCTION

Background on Energy Consumption in Computing
The global demand for computing devices—desktops, laptops, and servers—has surged, resulting in substantial energy consumption. Data centers alone account for around 1% of the world’s electricity use, a share likely to grow as digital activities expand. Reducing energy usage has become crucial for managing costs and minimizing environmental impact, particularly as devices run around the clock in homes, offices, and data centers worldwide.

Problem Statement
The main challenge is understanding how different computing devices consume energy under various conditions, from idle to peak load. This study addresses this issue by analyzing the power usage patterns of desktops, laptops, and servers, and by testing practical solutions for energy efficiency.

Purpose and Objectives
This research aims to measure and analyze energy consumption in desktops, laptops, and servers across different states—idle, low load, high load, and power-saving modes. Methods to enhance efficiency, such as reducing screen brightness, enabling power-saving settings, and optimizing processor performance, will be evaluated for their effectiveness in minimizing power consumption.

Significance of the Study
The findings will provide valuable insights for individuals, businesses, and data centers seeking to lower their energy expenses and reduce their environmental impact. By exploring practical solutions, this study contributes to sustainable computing practices and promotes more responsible energy use across the IT sector.
